Gaza Blockade

An Najah National University

The Gaza Strip has been plunged into a dire humanitarian crisis October 9, 2023
when Israel intensified its blockade following an attack by Hamas and subsequent warfare. Israeli Defense Minister Yoav Gallant announced a "total blockade," sealing off Gaza and demanding the return of hostages for its lifting, drawing international condemnation for collective punishment and potential war crimes. Critical shortages in essentials like food, water, medicine, and electricity emerged, exacerbated by Israeli obstruction of humanitarian aid delivery. Gaza's sole power plant closure led to darkness and infrastructure collapse, with catastrophic impacts on healthcare, sanitation, and the economy. Gazans face famine, disease outbreaks, and widespread suffering, while political tensions hinder international efforts for relief. Urgent action is imperative to lift the blockade, provide aid, and prevent further loss of life in one of the world's most besieged regions.
